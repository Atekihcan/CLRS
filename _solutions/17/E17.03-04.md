> What is the total cost of executing $$n$$ of the stack operations $$\textsc{Push}$$, $$\textsc{Pop}$$, and $$\textsc{Multipop}$$, assuming that the stack begins with $$s_0$$ objects and finishes with $$s_n$$ objects?

When the stack does not start empty, we cannot simply say the total cost is $$O(n)$$ because the initial objects on the stack could all be popped, adding cost that was not "prepaid" by earlier pushes in our sequence of $$n$$ operations. The potential method elegantly handles this situation.

Using the potential function $$\Phi(D_i) = s_i$$ (the number of objects in the stack after operation $$i$$), we showed in the text that:
- $$\textsc{Push}$$ has amortized cost 2
- $$\textsc{Pop}$$ has amortized cost 0
- $$\textsc{Multipop}$$ has amortized cost 0

The key equation from the potential method (equation 17.3 in the text) relates total actual cost to total amortized cost:

$$\sum_{i=1}^{n} c_i = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \hat{c}_i - \Phi(D_n) + \Phi(D_0)$$

Since each amortized cost $$\hat{c}_i \le 2$$ for all operations, we have:

$$\sum_{i=1}^{n} \hat{c}_i \le 2n$$

Substituting into the equation above:

$$\sum_{i=1}^{n} c_i \le 2n - s_n + s_0$$

If the stack shrinks ($$s_n < s_0$$), some of the initial objects were popped, and those pops contribute to the actual cost. The term $$s_0 - s_n$$ accounts for the "extra" work of popping items that were already on the stack before our sequence began.

If the stack grows ($$s_n > s_0$$), then $$s_n - s_0$$ objects were added overall. In this case, $$-s_n + s_0$$ is negative, which means our bound becomes tighter than $$2n$$, reflecting that we did not pop as many items as we could have.

We can also establish a lower bound. Since at least $$|s_n - s_0|$$ net operations must have occurred (either net pushes or net pops), and each such operation costs at least 1, we have a lower bound of $$\Omega(n + |s_n - s_0|)$$ in some cases. However, the question asks for the total cost, which is:

$$\boxed{O(n + s_0 - s_n) = O(n + s_0)}$$

More precisely, the total cost is at most $$2n - s_n + s_0$$ and at least $$n$$ (since each operation costs at least 1). When $$s_n \le s_0$$, the bound simplifies to $$O(n + s_0)$$ since the initial stack elements might all need to be popped.
