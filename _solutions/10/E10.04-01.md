> Draw the binary tree rooted at index 6 that is represented by the following attributes:

| index | key | left | right |
|-------|-----|------|-------|
| 1     | 12  | 7    | 3     |
| 2     | 15  | 8    | NIL   |
| 3     | 4   | 10   | NIL   |
| 4     | 10  | 5    | 9     |
| 5     | 2   | NIL  | NIL   |
| 6     | 18  | 1    | 4     |
| 7     | 7   | NIL  | NIL   |
| 8     | 14  | 6    | 2     |
| 9     | 21  | NIL  | NIL   |
| 10    | 5   | NIL  | NIL   |

To draw the tree, we start at the root (index 6) and follow the left and right pointers recursively.

Starting at index 6:
- Key: 18
- Left child: index 1
- Right child: index 4

Following index 1:
- Key: 12
- Left child: index 7
- Right child: index 3

Following index 7:
- Key: 7
- Left child: NIL
- Right child: NIL
- (This is a leaf)

Following index 3:
- Key: 4
- Left child: index 10
- Right child: NIL

Following index 10:
- Key: 5
- Left child: NIL
- Right child: NIL
- (This is a leaf)

Following index 4:
- Key: 10
- Left child: index 5
- Right child: index 9

Following index 5:
- Key: 2
- Left child: NIL
- Right child: NIL
- (This is a leaf)

Following index 9:
- Key: 21
- Left child: NIL
- Right child: NIL
- (This is a leaf)

The binary tree structure is:

```
                    18 (6)
                   /      \
                 12 (1)    10 (4)
                /   \      /    \
              7 (7)  4 (3) 2 (5) 21 (9)
                     /
                   5 (10)
```

Where the numbers in parentheses are the array indices.

Note that indices 2 and 8 are not part of this tree. They would be part of a different data structure or unused nodes in the array.
